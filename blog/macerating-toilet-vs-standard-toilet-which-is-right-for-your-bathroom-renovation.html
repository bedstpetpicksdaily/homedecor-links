What Is a Macerating Toilet?

A macerating toilet, also called an upflushing toilet, is a modern bathroom fixture that grinds waste and toilet paper into a fine slurry before pumping it through small-diameter pipes to your main sewer line or septic system. This innovative technology eliminates the need for traditional gravity-fed plumbing, making it ideal for basements, additions, and spaces where conventional toilets aren't practical. Simple Project's macerating toilets feature quiet operation, energy efficiency, and sleek designs that complement any bathroom aesthetic.

Understanding Standard Toilets

Standard toilets rely on gravity and water pressure to move waste through larger diameter pipes directly into your plumbing system. They're the traditional choice found in most homes and require proper slope and proximity to existing drain lines. While standard toilets are familiar and straightforward, they offer limited flexibility in bathroom placement and renovation scenarios. They work best in conventional bathroom layouts where main drain lines are easily accessible.

Key Differences in Installation

Installation differences between these toilet types are substantial. Standard toilets require careful placement near existing drain lines and must be positioned with the correct slope—typically 1/4 inch per foot. Macerating toilets offer revolutionary flexibility; they can be installed virtually anywhere since they pump waste upward and sideways through small, flexible tubing. This advantage makes them perfect for basement bathrooms, under-stair powder rooms, and creative renovation designs. Simple Project's bathroom fixtures come with comprehensive installation guides and smart features that make setup seamless for contractors and homeowners alike.

Cost Considerations for Your Renovation Budget

Budget is often the deciding factor in renovation projects. Standard toilets typically cost $200-$500 for quality models, with minimal installation expenses if plumbing is already in place. Macerating toilets range from $600-$1,500 depending on features and brand quality, but they can save thousands in plumbing reconfiguration costs. When factoring in potential drain line rerouting for a standard toilet versus the straightforward installation of a macerating unit, the total renovation cost often favors macerating options. Frequently Asked Questions

Can I install a macerating toilet in any bathroom?

Yes, macerating toilets can be installed almost anywhere since they pump waste through small-diameter pipes. This makes them ideal for basements, attics, additions, and spaces where standard plumbing isn't feasible. Simple Project's macerating toilets are designed for maximum installation flexibility.

Do macerating toilets require electricity?

Yes, macerating toilets require a power outlet nearby to operate the grinding and pumping motor. This is typically a standard 110-volt outlet positioned close to the toilet, making it a minor consideration in most renovation projects.

Are macerating toilets noisy?

Modern macerating toilets, including those from Simple Project, operate much more quietly than older models. Many feature advanced insulation and noise-reduction technology, making them nearly silent during operation.

What's the maintenance difference between macerating and standard toilets?

Macerating toilets require slightly more maintenance due to their mechanical components. However, Simple Project's bathroom fixtures are engineered for durability and reliability, requiring only routine inspections and standard toilet cleaning practices.

How much can I save by choosing a macerating toilet for my renovation?

While macerating toilets cost more upfront ($600-$1,500), they typically save $1,000-$3,000+ in plumbing installation costs by eliminating expensive drain line modifications, making them cost-effective for most renovations.
