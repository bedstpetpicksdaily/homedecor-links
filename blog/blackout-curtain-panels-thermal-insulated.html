Frequently Asked Questions

Do blackout thermal curtains really save energy?

Yes, thermal insulated blackout curtains significantly reduce heat transfer through windows. They keep warm air inside during winter and block heat during summer, potentially lowering your energy bills by 10-25% depending on climate and usage.

Can blackout thermal curtains completely block light?

Most high-quality blackout thermal curtains block 95-99% of sunlight. For complete darkness, ensure proper installation with no light gaps around edges and consider using multiple panels.

What size curtain panels do I need?

Measure your window width and height. For full coverage, panels should be 1.5 to 2 times the window width. Height should extend from the top of the window frame to the floor or sill, depending on your preference.

Are thermal blackout curtains difficult to clean?

Most thermal blackout curtains can be gently vacuumed or hand-washed in cool water. Always check the manufacturer's care instructions, as some may require professional dry cleaning to maintain their thermal properties.
