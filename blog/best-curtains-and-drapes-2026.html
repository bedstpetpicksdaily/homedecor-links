Buying Guide: What to Look For in Curtains and Drapes

  • Light Control & Privacy: Determine whether you need blackout curtains for complete darkness, room-darkening for filtered light, or sheer curtains for brightness. Consider your room's purpose—bedrooms need more darkness than living rooms.
  • Material & Durability: Choose between cotton, polyester, linen, or blends based on durability and maintenance needs. Synthetic blends resist wrinkles and fading, while natural fibers offer elegance but require more care.
  • Thermal Efficiency & Energy Savings: Look for insulated or thermal curtains to regulate room temperature year-round. These can reduce heating and cooling costs while improving comfort and reducing outside noise.

Frequently Asked Questions About Curtains and Drapes

What's the difference between curtains and drapes?
Curtains are typically lightweight fabric panels used for decoration and light filtering, while drapes are heavier, more formal window coverings that provide better insulation and light blocking. Many people use these terms interchangeably today, though drapes traditionally refer to floor-length, lined fabric panels.

How do I measure for new curtains and drapes?
Measure the width of your window and multiply by 1.5 to 2 for proper fullness. For length, measure from the top of the rod to where you want the curtains to end—typically just touching the floor or windowsill. Always measure multiple spots as windows aren't always perfectly square.
