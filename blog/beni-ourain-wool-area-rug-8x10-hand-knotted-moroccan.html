Frequently Asked Questions

How does this compare to synthetic Moroccan-style rugs?

Wool holds its structure indefinitely; synthetic versions start matting within 6-12 months in living rooms. I tested a polyester alternative side-by-side, and the difference was immediate. Wool also naturally resists staining better because the fibers are less porous. The real trade-off is price—synthetics cost 40-60% less upfront but need replacing sooner.

Will this rug shed, and for how long?

Expect light shedding for the first 2-4 weeks; I noticed this most when vacuuming. After that, shedding drops to almost nothing. Hand-knotted rugs are tighter than tufted ones, so loose fibers are minimal compared to budget alternatives. A lint roller helps during that initial period, but it's not excessive.

Is hand-knotted worth the premium over hand-tufted?

Yes, measurably. Hand-knotted means each knot is individually tied to the base, creating a reversible, durable rug that lasts 20+ years. Hand-tufted uses a gun and backing adhesive, which eventually weakens. For an 8x10 investment, the hand-knotted construction justifies the higher cost—it's essentially buying durability upfront rather than replacing every 5-7 years.
