Frequently Asked Questions

How bright is this lamp, and what room is it best for?

This is accent lighting, not task lighting. It won't properly light a workspace, but it's perfect for bedrooms, living room side tables, and reading nooks where you want mood over brightness. The linen shade significantly diffuses the bulb's output, so expect about 40% of what a bare bulb would give. That's exactly the point—it's designed to feel warm and calming.

Can you use any light bulb, or does it need something specific?

Standard E26 base bulbs work fine. You have freedom here. Use LED for cool efficiency and minimal heat, or go incandescent if you prefer warmer color. Most people stick LED because the lamp stays cooler and reduces fire risk if the shade ever gets bumped. The wattage limit is typically 60W, which is plenty for the shade and ceramic base.

Is the cord long enough, or will you need an extension?

Standard cord length is around 5–6 feet, which covers most nightstand-to-outlet situations. If your outlet is further away (like across a bedroom), you'll want an extension cord. Plan for this before ordering if your room layout is tricky. It's a minor consideration but worth thinking through so you're not frustratingly short.

How does this compare to more expensive mid-century ceramic lamps?

The main difference at higher price points is usually thicker ceramic and branded designer labels. Functionally and aesthetically, this Rivet lamp delivers 85% of what you'd get at double the price. The shade quality is the real standout—it's not thin or plasticky. If you're budget-conscious but care about aesthetics, this hits the sweet spot.

Will the ceramic base chip or discolor over time?

Ceramic holds up well if treated normally. Avoid knocking it around, and you'll be fine for years. Discoloration isn't typical unless it sits directly in sunlight constantly—ceramic can yellow slightly with UV exposure, same as linen. Keep it out of direct sun, and you won't notice aging.
