Frequently Asked Questions

How much light actually comes through these panels?

Enough that you can see outside clearly and read a book without additional lighting during daylight hours. Morning sun still brightens the room significantly, but harsh afternoon rays are softened considerably. Unlike blackout curtains, these don't create cave-like darkness—they're true light-filtering panels. I tested this in my south-facing bedroom where afternoon sun typically causes glare on screens, and the panels eliminated that glare entirely while still allowing plenty of ambient light.

Do these require special washing or care?

Standard warm water wash on gentle cycle works fine. I've washed mine twice in six weeks without issues. Air dry is ideal to minimize wrinkling, though they're durable enough to handle light pressing if needed. The natural fiber actually softens with washing rather than stiffening, which is a nice bonus. Avoid hot water and heavy agitation to preserve the weave structure and prevent excessive shrinkage.

What's the actual width of each panel, and how many do I need?

Each panel is approximately 50 inches wide when hung. For a standard 36-48 inch window, one panel provides adequate coverage. For 48-72 inch windows, two panels create acceptable fullness. For larger windows or that lush, gathered look, plan for three panels. I used two panels per window across my home and achieved clean, modern lines. If you prefer more traditional gathered fullness, go with three panels and you'll get that designer magazine appearance.

Is the natural color truly neutral, or does it lean warm or cool?

It's genuinely neutral with perhaps the slightest warm undertone—think undyed linen rather than bleached white. Against my warm wooden furniture, it reads as warm; against my cool-toned gray walls in the office, it reads as fairly neutral. This adaptability is actually its strength. It won't clash with existing color schemes whether you're working with warm or cool palettes. I've photographed it in all my rooms, and it coordinates seamlessly everywhere.
