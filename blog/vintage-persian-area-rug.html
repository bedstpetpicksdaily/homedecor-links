Frequently Asked Questions

How can I tell if a Persian rug is authentic?

Authentic vintage Persian rugs feature hand-knotted construction with irregular patterns, natural dyes that may fade slightly, and a signature or weaver's mark on the back. Check the density of knots, examine the fringe attachment, and consult with certified appraisers for valuable pieces.

What's the difference between Persian and Oriental rugs?

Persian rugs originate specifically from Iran and feature distinctive regional patterns and color palettes. Oriental is a broader category including rugs from Turkey, Afghanistan, and other regions. Persian rugs are prized for their heritage, intricate designs, and superior craftsmanship.

How do I care for a vintage Persian rug?

Vacuum gently with a low-pile setting, rotate quarterly to ensure even wear, and have it professionally cleaned every 3-5 years using traditional methods. Avoid direct sunlight, keep it away from moisture, and use rug pads underneath to prevent slipping and damage.

Are vintage Persian rugs suitable for high-traffic areas?

While durable, vintage Persian rugs are best placed in moderate-traffic areas like living rooms or bedrooms. Heavy foot traffic and families with young children may risk premature wear, so consider placement carefully before purchasing.
