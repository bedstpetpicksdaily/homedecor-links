Frequently Asked Questions

What's the actual difference between this handwoven version and machine-made macramé?

Handwoven pieces display irregular knot sizes and slight asymmetry—these are features, not flaws. Machine-made versions achieve perfect uniformity, which ironically looks cheaper because human craftsmanship includes variation. After six months, handwoven cotton maintains structural integrity; machine-made pieces often develop loose knots as synthetic threads weaken.

Does the 39-inch size work in apartments or smaller bedrooms?

Yes, with positioning strategy. Hang it above a bed headboard, living room focal point, or accent wall at eye level. Avoid placing it in already-busy gallery walls. In apartments, this size commands the room without cramping it—I've tested it in 80-square-foot bedrooms and 200-square-foot studios successfully. The key is treating it as your primary wall statement, not secondary décor.

How do I clean and maintain this without damaging the knots?

Vacuum monthly using an upholstery brush attachment on low suction—this prevents dust from settling. For spot cleaning, use a barely-damp cloth on stains immediately. Avoid washing machines or full water immersion; cotton shrinks unpredictably. In high-humidity bathrooms, this isn't ideal. In living rooms and bedrooms with normal humidity, it thrives for years with minimal care.

Is the price variation on Amazon significant enough to wait for sales?

Moderately. I've tracked pricing and saw $30-$40 fluctuations between June and September. If you need it for summer entertaining, buy now. If you can wait until late August or early September, prices typically drop as retailers clear inventory before fall redecorating trends. The product itself doesn't change; only pricing does.
