Why Choose a Macerating Toilet for Your Basement?

Macerating toilets are game-chaners for basement bathrooms because they don't require traditional gravity-based drainage. Instead, they use a macerator pump to grind waste and toilet paper into a fine slurry, which is then pumped through a small-diameter discharge line to your home's main sewage system or septic tank. This means you can install a toilet almost anywhere in your basement—even below the main sewer line—without costly excavation or plumbing work. Simple Project's macerating toilets are engineered with quiet operation and advanced waste-grinding technology, making them ideal for both residential and light commercial applications.

Frequently Asked Questions

How loud is a Simple Project macerating toilet?

Simple Project macerating toilets are engineered for quiet operation, typically producing 70-75 decibels during flushing—comparable to a standard toilet. Advanced sound-dampening technology in their 2026 models reduces noise even further compared to older macerating systems.

Can I use regular toilet paper with a Simple Project macerating toilet?

Yes, Simple Project macerating toilets are designed to handle standard toilet paper. The powerful grinding mechanism breaks down waste and paper efficiently. However, avoid flushing non-biodegradable items like wet wipes or feminine hygiene products, which can damage the pump.

What is the typical lifespan of a Simple Project macerating toilet?

With proper maintenance, Simple Project macerating toilets typically last 10-15 years. The brand offers comprehensive warranties and readily available replacement parts. Regular filter cleaning and professional inspections every 1-2 years help extend the unit's lifespan.

Do Simple Project macerating toilets work with septic systems?

Yes, most Simple Project macerating toilets are compatible with septic systems. The small-diameter discharge line and reduced water flow can actually benefit septic tank function. Always verify compatibility with your specific septic system before installation.

How much does it cost to install a Simple Project macerating toilet?

Installation costs vary by location and plumbing complexity, typically ranging from $1,000-$2,500. This is significantly less than traditional basement bathroom plumbing, which can exceed $5,000-$10,000 in excavation and drain relocation costs.
