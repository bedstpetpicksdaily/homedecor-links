Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to build an EWA wooden mechanical model kit?

Assembly time varies by model complexity, typically ranging from 8 to 30+ hours. Beginner kits may take 5-10 hours, while advanced mechanical models can require 20-30+ hours of focused building time. The detailed instruction manuals guide you through each step at your own pace.

Are EWA wooden model kits suitable for children?

EWA offers age-appropriate kits for various skill levels. Younger children (ages 8+) should start with beginner models and work with adult supervision, while teens and adults can tackle more complex mechanical kits. Their STEM models are specifically designed to be educational and engaging for school-age children.

What makes EWA's wooden kits eco-friendly?

EWA uses sustainably sourced wood and practices responsible forestry. Their materials are biodegradable, chemical-free, and sourced from responsibly managed forests. The precision laser-cutting process minimizes waste, and all packaging is recyclable or compostable.

Do I need special tools to assemble an EWA wooden model kit?

No. EWA kits come with pre-cut pieces that require no saws or power tools. You may only need basic tools like sandpaper (often included), wood glue, and occasionally a screwdriver. Detailed instructions explain exactly what's needed.

Can finished wooden mechanical models function after assembly?

Yes. Many EWA mechanical models feature working mechanical functions—gears, wheels, and automata that actually move. These functional elements are what make them unique decorative pieces that combine beauty with interactive appeal.
