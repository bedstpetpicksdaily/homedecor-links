Frequently Asked Questions

What material is this macramé actually made from?

Based on the price point and category, this is almost certainly synthetic cord designed to mimic jute—likely polyester or acrylic blend. That sounds cheap on paper, but it's actually a plus: synthetic cords resist moisture, won't attract mildew in humid climates (like summer kitchens), and clean easily with a gentle vacuum or soft brush. Real jute is beautiful but demands more care and fades faster in sunlight. For a $10 piece, synthetic is the practical choice.

How do I hang it, and will it damage my walls?

Macramé tapestries typically come with a dowel rod at the top and a loop or hook attachment. You'll need a single wall hook or picture nail—nothing more. No drywall anchors required unless your walls are textured plaster. It won't damage paint or drywall when removed, making it ideal for renters. The weight is negligible (under 2 pounds for a 'large' boho piece), so even a basic plastic toggle bolt holds securely.

Will this look cheap compared to higher-priced macramé?

Honestly? From three feet away, probably not. The difference between a $10 and a $60 macramé tapestry shows up in knot precision, weight, and cord quality—none of which are obvious from casual viewing distance. Where you'll notice the gap is in longevity and tactile feel. If you run your hand over it or examine the knots up close, budget macramé feels slightly stiff or plasticky. For wall art you're looking at, not touching, the value gap shrinks significantly.
