Frequently Asked Questions

Is the ceramic base glazed or unglazed, and does it feel cheap?

The base is glazed ceramic with a smooth finish that actually feels substantive in your hands. It's not delicate—this is a lamp that won't wobble on an uneven nightstand, which matters more than most reviews mention. Unglazed ceramic bases feel lighter and more fragile; this one has weight and intention.

What's the actual brightness output? Is this pure ambiance or functional task lighting?

This is ambient-forward lighting, not task lighting. The linen shade softens the bulb output deliberately, so if you're using this as your sole bedroom light source for reading, you'll be disappointed. It's perfect for bedside atmosphere or as secondary lighting paired with a brighter fixture. Budget lamps with bare bulbs or paper shades will be brighter at the same wattage.

How does this compare to similar-looking lamps from Target or Article?

You'll find ceramic bases and linen shades at Target ($60-90) and Article ($120-180) with nearly identical aesthetics. The Schoolhouse Electric version typically costs more, justifiable if you value their design curation and slightly more refined proportions. Will the linen shade yellow over time from heat or sun exposure?

Yes, and this is worth knowing upfront. Linen shades exposed to direct sunlight or used with high-wattage bulbs will gradually yellow. Using LED bulbs (recommended anyway for energy savings) and keeping this lamp away from south-facing windows minimizes the issue, but expect some aging over 2-3 years of regular use.

Is this lamp available in other shade colors or base finishes?

Schoolhouse Electric offers variations depending on current inventory, but the classic ceramic base with linen shade is their signature combination. Check their site directly rather than assuming Amazon carries all colorways—third-party sellers sometimes list limited options, and prices vary significantly based on which retailer stocks what.
