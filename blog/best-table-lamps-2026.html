Buying Guide: What to Look For in a Table Lamp

Choosing the right table lamp involves considering several important factors:

  • Brightness and Light Color: Look for adjustable brightness levels and color temperature options. Warm light (2700K) creates a cozy atmosphere, while cool light (5000K) is better for focused work and reading.
  • Size and Style: Consider the lamp's height, base diameter, and shade design in relation to your furniture and room décor. A good rule is that your lamp should be roughly one-quarter to one-third the height of the furniture it sits on.
  • Energy Efficiency and Features: LED bulbs consume 75% less energy than incandescent bulbs and last longer. Additional features like USB charging ports, touch controls, and dimmable settings add convenience and value.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the ideal height for a table lamp?

Most table lamps should be 24-34 inches tall from base to top of the shade. When seated, the bottom of the lamp shade should be at or slightly below eye level to avoid glare and provide proper illumination without obstruction.

Can table lamps be used as primary lighting in a room?

While table lamps can supplement room lighting, they work best as accent or task lighting rather than primary room lighting. For adequate illumination in a bedroom or living room, combine table lamps with ceiling fixtures, floor lamps, or wall sconces.
