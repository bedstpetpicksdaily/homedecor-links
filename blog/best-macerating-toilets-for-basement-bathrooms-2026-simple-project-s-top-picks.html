What Are Macerating Toilets and Why Choose Them for Basements?

Macerating toilets, also known as upflush toilets, are compact units that grind waste and paper into a fine slurry before pumping it upward to your home's main sewer line or septic system. This innovative design is particularly ideal for basement bathrooms where traditional gravity-fed plumbing isn't feasible. Simple Project's macerating systems integrate this technology with modern smart features, allowing homeowners to install full bathrooms in basements without breaking through concrete foundations or creating extensive new drainage pathways.

The primary advantage of macerating toilets is their flexibility in placement. Unlike conventional toilets that require gravity-assisted drainage, these units can be positioned virtually anywhere in your basement, giving you complete design freedom for your bathroom layout. Simple Project's engineered solutions are built with quiet operation in mind, utilizing advanced sound-dampening technology so you won't hear grinding or pumping sounds during use.

Do macerating toilets require electricity?

Yes, macerating toilets use an electric motor to grind and pump waste. Simple Project's models require a dedicated electrical outlet, which is typically installed near the toilet location during setup.

Simple Project's advanced sound-dampening technology keeps noise levels comparable to standard toilets. Most users report hearing minimal grinding or pumping sounds during normal operation.
