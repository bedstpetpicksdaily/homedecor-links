Frequently Asked Questions

How bright are these LED heads compared to halogen track lights?

Each head outputs approximately 400 lumens, which is equivalent to a 40-50 watt halogen bulb. That's bright enough for accent lighting and task work but not meant to be your primary room lighting. In my office, I use the Orion for wall art illumination while a ceiling fixture handles overall brightness. They work in complementary layers, not as standalone solutions.

Will this work in a rental apartment?

Yes, with caveats. The installation requires drilling into drywall and connecting to your light switch circuit, which most landlords won't approve permanently. However, if you're comfortable leaving the light switch connection in place and patching holes before move-out, many renters successfully install these. Some have also mounted the track to wooden beams or ledges without permanent installation, though that limits positioning options.

How does the Brightech Orion compare to Track Master or other mid-range brands?

The Orion competes directly with Track Master and Kichler budget lines. Brightech's advantage is the included dimmer compatibility and 330-degree rotation—many competitors require separate dimmer purchases and offer less head mobility. You'll pay $30-50 more for the Orion, but that translates to fewer compatibility headaches. For under $150 total investment (before professional installation), the feature set is hard to beat. Premium brands like Juno cost 2-3x more and target professional installations.
