Frequently Asked Questions

Will this rug shed everywhere for months?

Expect noticeable shedding for the first 2-3 weeks, particularly if you vacuum daily or have dark clothing stored nearby. This is the loose fibers from the tufting process releasing. After that window, shedding drops dramatically. I'd recommend vacuuming at least three times a week initially, then backing off to weekly maintenance. The shedding phase is annoying but temporary and doesn't indicate a defective rug.

How does this handle pet accidents or serious stains?

Wool naturally resists staining better than synthetics, but it's not magic. Fresh liquid spills should be blotted immediately—the chunky loops help absorb without spreading as quickly as flat rugs. For pet accidents, enzyme-based cleaners work well on wool. I tested this with a wine spill and caught it early; it came out completely. A month-old mystery stain from my toddler proved more stubborn. Professional cleaning is recommended annually if you have pets or kids, which factors into the true ownership cost.

Is the hand-tufted construction noticeably better than machine-made?
