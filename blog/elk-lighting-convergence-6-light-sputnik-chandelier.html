Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Elk Lighting Convergence work with standard dimmer switches?

Not automatically. The fixture itself is compatible with dimmable bulbs, but you need to pair it with dimmable LED candelabra bulbs specifically rated for dimmer use. Standard incandescent bulbs work fine with traditional dimmers, but LEDs need compatibility verification. Check the bulb packaging before purchasing—it's usually marked clearly. If you have an existing dimmer, confirm it supports the bulb type you choose.

What's the actual brightness output with six bulbs?

That depends entirely on bulb wattage. With six 40-watt candelabra equivalents (typically around 400 lumens each), you're looking at roughly 2400 total lumens—enough for general room lighting in a 12x14 space. For larger rooms, this works best as primary overhead lighting paired with task lighting. Test dimming at 50% and 100% brightness; most users find 75% hits the sweet spot for evening ambiance without harsh shadows.

How does the brass finish hold up over time compared to chrome or matte black?

Brass is genuinely forgiving. It naturally patinas slightly, which either looks intentional or you can polish it back to shine—your choice. Chrome shows fingerprints constantly and requires frequent cleaning. Matte black hides dust better but feels more cold in most interiors. After six weeks of testing, the brass finish required only occasional wiping and actually improved in appearance as it naturally aged. It's the most maintenance-balanced option if you're in a humid climate.
