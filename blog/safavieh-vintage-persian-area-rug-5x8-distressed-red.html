Frequently Asked Questions

How does this compare to authentic Persian rugs?

It doesn't. Authentic hand-knotted Persian rugs cost $1,500–$5,000+ for this size. Safavieh's Vintage line mimics the visual aesthetic through distressing and color variation, but the construction is machine-loomed polypropylene. Think of it as the visual inspiration without the investment—suitable for renters and young families who might upgrade later.

Will this rug shed? How much maintenance does it need?

Minimal shedding after the first 2–3 weeks of vacuuming. Ongoing maintenance is simple: vacuum weekly (medium pile traps dust), spot-clean spills immediately with cool water and mild detergent. Avoid steam cleaning—excessive heat can warp polypropylene fibers. A quality rug pad underneath prevents slipping and protects hardwood.

What's the actual color—is it true red or more burgundy?

It's a deep rust-red leaning toward burgundy, not a bright fire-engine red. The distressing adds lighter coral and rust tones throughout, so it reads as more sophisticated and less bold than marketing photos sometimes suggest. This works in its favor if you're worried about overwhelming a space.

How does it hold up with kids and pets?

Polypropylene is stain-resistant and easy to clean, which makes it pet-friendly for dogs. However, cats that urine-mark or heavily shedding pets are problematic—the fiber retains odor. For households with young children, it's excellent; spills wipe away without panic.
