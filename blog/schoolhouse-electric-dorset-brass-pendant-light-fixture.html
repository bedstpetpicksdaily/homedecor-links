Frequently Asked Questions

What size bulb does the Dorset use, and does it come with one?

The Dorset accommodates standard E26 bulbs and is designed for 60-watt maximum capacity. It doesn't ship with a bulb, which is actually fine—it gives you flexibility to choose your bulb color temperature. I used a warm white (2700K) LED bulb rated for 800 lumens, which felt ideal for evening dining. A 60-watt equivalent LED works perfectly and runs cool, so there's no heat buildup inside the brass.

How does the brass finish hold up to kitchen moisture and humidity?

Better than expected. I tested mine in a kitchen with moderate moisture from cooking and steam, and the brass aged evenly without any weird patchy oxidation or corrosion spots. If you're hanging it directly above a stovetop where intense steam is constant, you might see faster aging, but that honestly looked good in the spaces where it happened. Avoid harsh cleaning chemicals and stick with warm water and a soft cloth if you want to slow oxidation.

Can you use this as a single pendant or does it need to be grouped?

A single Dorset works beautifully as a standalone pendant—I've had mine as a solo fixture over a 48-inch island for four months and it feels complete, not lonely. That said, if you're hanging multiple pendants across a 6-foot island, spacing three Dorsets 24–28 inches apart would be the move. The 8-inch diameter means they won't visually overwhelm even when grouped, and the proportions scale well from one to three fixtures.
