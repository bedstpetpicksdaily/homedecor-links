Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between soy, beeswax, and paraffin candles?

Soy wax (made from soybeans) and beeswax burn cleaner and longer than paraffin, produce less soot, and are more eco-friendly. They're ideal for décor-focused homes where you want minimal environmental impact. Paraffin is cheaper but has shorter burn times and can discolor walls.

How do I choose a candle scent that complements my home décor?

Consider your room's style and purpose. Minimalist spaces pair well with fresh, clean scents (citrus, sea salt). Bohemian rooms suit warm, spiced fragrances (amber, sandalwood). Match the candle's vessel color and material to your existing pillows, rugs, and wall art for cohesive design.

Why do some candles cost so much more than others?

Premium candles use higher-quality wax blends, fragrance concentrations, and hand-poured vessels. They burn longer, smell stronger, and look better—making them better investments for home décor. A $50 candle burning 60 hours offers better value than a $15 candle burning 20 hours.

Can I leave candles burning unattended?

No. Always extinguish candles before leaving a room or going to sleep. Follow the manufacturer's guidelines (typically 3-4 hour maximum burns) and trim wicks to prevent excessive smoking and soot buildup on walls and ceilings.

How do I prevent candle tunneling?

Tunneling occurs when the center burns down while wax remains on the sides. To prevent it, burn candles for 2-3 hours on first use to melt the entire wax surface, and always trim wicks to ¼ inch before lighting.
