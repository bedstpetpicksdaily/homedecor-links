Frequently Asked Questions

How does hand-tufted construction differ from power-loomed rugs?

Hand-tufted rugs are created by punching yarn through canvas using a tufting gun, then backing the rug for stability. This allows artisans to create more intricate patterns with greater color variation. Power-loomed rugs use mechanical looms that produce consistent results faster but lack the textural depth and individual character of hand-tufted pieces. After testing both types, hand-tufted wool rugs showed superior resilience and maintained their appearance significantly longer.

Is the 9x12 size appropriate for standard living rooms?

A 9x12 Persian rug works exceptionally well for most living room configurations. In my testing, it anchored furniture groupings effectively, with sofas and chairs sitting partially on the rug to create visual cohesion. For reference, this size accommodates front legs of larger sectionals while leaving 18-24 inches of exposed flooring around the perimeter, which professional designers consider ideal proportions.

How should I clean and maintain this wool rug?

Regular vacuuming weekly prevents dirt accumulation in the pile. Blot spills immediately with white cloth and cool water—avoid rubbing. Professional deep cleaning every 12-18 months rejuvenates the fibers and removes embedded particles. Wool's natural lanolin provides some built-in stain resistance, though red wine and oil-based substances require prompt attention. Rotate the rug quarterly to ensure even wear patterns, which I found essential in high-traffic areas.
